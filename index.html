- The Cowichan Tribes are a First Nations tribe located on Vancouver Island, British Columbia, Canada. Their traditional territory stretches from the southern tip of Saanich Inlet to the northern part of Cowichan Lake. The term "Cowichan" means "warm valley" in the Hul'qumi'num language, reflecting the region's climate and fertile land. The Cowichan Tribes Band is the largest First Nations band in British Columbia, with over 4,900 registered members. The art of weaving, particularly the creation of Cowichan sweaters, is a significant cultural practice of the Cowichan Tribes. The Hul'qumi'num language is central to their cultural traditions and efforts for its revitalization and preservation are ongoing. The Cowichan Tribes celebrate their heritage through events like the annual Powwow, which features dancing, singing, drumming, and storytelling.
